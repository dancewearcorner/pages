Executive Summary
What the board needs to know

Four findings.
One sequenced plan.

Finding 01

List health is at risk from over-mailing.

We send to the same recipients an average of 3.6 times per week — above the 2–3/week recommended ceiling in nearly every week of the period. Click-through rate sits at 1.21%, below the 2.5–3% retail benchmark. Engagement-driven inbox placement is the next domino.

Finding 02

Welcome Series is silently churning existing customers.

The Existing-Customer branch of our New Welcome Series shows a 5.8% unsubscribe rate — roughly 10–20× a healthy benchmark. We are treating returning buyers like brand-new subscribers, and they are leaving.

Finding 03

Flows are punching above their weight.

Four core flows generated $148,961 from a fraction of total volume. Abandoned Cart alone delivered $73,484 from 9,027 recipients — $8.14 per recipient. With timing fixes and copy refresh, this can grow materially.

Finding 04

The biggest segmentation win is hiding in plain sight.

All-time category buyers receiving a replenishment-style offer convert at the highest rate of any audience tested. The strategic answer is fewer broadsides and more targeted, recency-aware sends.

The Welcome Series Risk
We're churning our most valuable cohort

The Welcome Series risk.

5.8%
unsubscribe rate on the Existing-Customer branch of New Welcome Series — roughly 10–20× a healthy welcome unsubscribe rate.
What we're seeing

Existing customers are exiting on the welcome path.

The Existing-Customer branch of the New Welcome Series shows a 5.8% unsubscribe rate — versus ~0.3–0.5% on a healthy welcome flow. Recipients on this branch are receiving "Welcome to DanceWear Corner!" content despite already being known to the brand, and many opt out rather than re-introduce themselves.

Note: confirming exactly how existing customers are entering this branch (new opt-ins, re-subscribers, or list imports) is a Week 1 diagnostic — see Action Plan.

What we recommend

Split the flow into two distinct paths.

New Subscriber → standard 5–6 email welcome. Existing Customer → short acknowledgment sequence with a tailored category recommendation rather than a brand introduction. Done in roughly one sprint of work, after we confirm the entry path.

Recommended Actions

Recommended action plan.

Quick Wins

This week

  • Split Welcome Series into New Subscriber and Existing Customer paths (highest priority).
  • Pause or rewrite "Copy of Email #2" in Win Back and "Copy of Email #1" in Browse Abandonment.
  • Suppress contacts >12 months lapsed from broadcast list; route to Reactivation instead.
30-Day

Within the next month

  • Reduce broadcast send cadence to ≤2–3 per week per recipient via segmentation rules.
  • Strengthen the offer or urgency on Abandoned Cart Email #3 (test 10–15% off).
  • Audit and verify trigger windows and inter-message delays on all four flows.
  • Build a recurring all-time-buyer + replenishment-offer campaign cadence.
60–90 Day

Strategic

  • Trim Welcome Series from 12 to 5–6 messages with category-led content.
  • Stand up a formal Reactivation flow for 12+ month lapsed contacts.
  • Move Birthday email out of Welcome into a dedicated annual flow.
  • Begin email content review and copy refresh on the four core flows.
